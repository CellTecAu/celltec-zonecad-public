Welcome to ZoneCAD

ZoneCAD is CellTec's in-browser plan-view designer for modular fencing and zoning. Lay out posts, link them with panels, doors and gates, mark out zones and labels — then pull a live parts list (BOM) or export the drawing to DXF or PNG.

Under the hood is a complete 2-D geometric constraint solver. Every constraint confines a post to a locus — a line (align, edge tie, bearing, collinear) or a circle (dimension) — and the solver places each post where its loci intersect. Any pairing works: polar placement (angle + distance), trilateration (two distances), a set distance along a line. The freer post is the one that moves, read-only reference dimensions show in (brackets), and anything the solver can't satisfy turns red instead of being hidden. Outlined value pills are editable — blue for post-to-post dimensions, violet ▭ for panel widths; plain violet pills are the derived per-panel dimensions.

New here? Open Examples ▾ in the toolbar: the Constraints Sample has one of every constraint to experiment with, alongside dimensioned hinged-door, sliding-door and cantilever-gate layouts.

ZoneCAD — Features & Guide

ZoneCAD is an in-browser plan-view designer for modular fencing and zoning: place posts, link them with panels/doors/gates, add zones and labels, then export a parts list or DXF. Everything below is currently implemented.

Tools (left toolbox)

Select (V)
Click to select; drag a post, zone or label to move it. Drag empty space to box-select posts. A single selected post shows a rotation handle. Click the rendered leaf/track of a door or gate to select it, not just its centreline.
Move (G)
Like Select, but tuned for repositioning: object snapping (hinge pins / span lines) and the edit handles are off, so posts don't jump onto brackets. Post-to-post alignment guides, grid snap and Shift-ortho still apply.
Add Post (A)
Click to drop a post; it auto-links to its nearest unconnected neighbour with a panel. Stays active for placing several.
Add Bollard (B)
Click to place a bollard — 165×3.2 CHS post with a welded cap on a 220 OD circular baseplate (4×Ø17 at 192.5 PCD). Constrains exactly like a post (align, dimension, angle, tie, lock) but never links with panels.
Markup
Click to place free-floating text; type it inline. Double-click to re-edit, drag to move, Delete to remove.
Measure (R)
Click two points — snaps to posts and bracket pins — for a live distance readout. Non-destructive.
Rectangular / Circular Zone (Z / C)
Drag out a named zone region for planning areas; editable size and colour.
Trace (T)
Sketch a rough shape freehand — one stroke per wall run. ZoneCAD snaps every edge to the nearest angle step (Settings, default 45°), places a post at each corner, and links panels between them. Leave a gap between two strokes and it becomes a doorway with the flanking posts kept aligned. Draw as many strokes as you like, then tap ✓ Done (or Enter). The layout auto-sizes around the shape; the first dimension you then set rescales the whole trace to real size. Alignment and angle constraints are added automatically so the shape holds its form.
Split (S)
Click anywhere along a panel to insert a post there, splitting it into two panels that inherit the original's settings. The tool stays active, so you can keep clicking to subdivide a run.
Add Panel (P)
Creates a panel between two or more selected posts (nearest valid pair).
Flip Mesh
Flips which face the mesh sits on for the selected panels.
Panel Width (W)
Click a panel and type its physical width — the ▭ constraint holds it as the post pivots on its hinge pin. Values above the max panel run (Zone Configuration, default 2400) are rejected: a longer run needs multiple panels, so dimension post-to-post instead.
Lock (K)
Pins the selected post(s) at their current position (removes both degrees of freedom).

Constraints

Constraints hold posts in relationship to each other or the layout, and re-solve automatically when a parent moves (dependency graph with cycle protection). Active constraints show as lettered badges around a post.

How it works — degrees of freedom

Every post has 2 degrees of freedom (its X and Y), and each constraint confines it to a locus — a line or a circle — removing one DOF. Two constraints fully define a post; the solver places it where the two loci meet.

  • Line locus (removes 1 DOF): Align H (a horizontal line), Align V (vertical), Tie to Edge (a line at the edge), Collinear (the line through two other posts, any angle), Angle (a ray at a bearing). A post with just one of these slides along that line.
  • Circle locus (removes 1 DOF): Dimension — a fixed distance from another post. A post with just one swings on that circle.
  • Lock removes both DOF (a fixed datum).

Because it solves the two loci together, any pairing works:

  • Line + Circle — e.g. Align V + Dimension ("same X as A, 1500 mm from B"), or Collinear + Dimension (a set distance along a line).
  • Angle + Dimension = polar placement — "30° and 1000 mm from A".
  • Two Dimensions = trilateration — positioned by its distance to two posts (two circles meet at two points; the nearer one to where the post currently sits is chosen, so you can drag to pick the side).
  • Line + Line — two lines meet at a point (e.g. Collinear + Align, or two Angles).

A third constraint, or two parallel lines that can't both hold (e.g. two Align V), is refused with a reason. A pairing that has no solution (circles too far apart, a distance shorter than the line reaches) isn't blocked — it's placed as close as possible and flagged red.

Rules of thumb

  • Anchor a corner. Leave one post free (no constraints) and reference everything back to it. Constraints can't form a loop (A→B→…→A) — if one is refused with "would create a constraint loop", flip a constraint's direction so nothing points back to the anchor.
  • The freer post moves. When you constrain two posts, the tool automatically makes the less-constrained one the one that moves, so it won't yank a post that already carries a dimension. For 3+ posts it anchors the most-constrained one.
  • If a constraint can't be satisfied it turns red and the status bar shows how many are unsatisfied — nothing fails silently. Refused constraints explain why in a toast.

Colour legend

  • Blue — a driving dimension/angle that's satisfied.
  • Violet — a reference (driven, read-only) dimension: shows the live value in parentheses, never moves posts.
  • Red — broken: the solver couldn't meet it (label shows target → actual).
Align Horizontal (H)
Give posts the same Y. With 2+ posts selected they align directly (the freer one moves); with one, pick a reference in the dialog.
Align Vertical (V)
Same X, same selection rules as above.
Tie to Edge (E) — double-click its dashed line to edit the distance
Fix a post a set distance from a layout edge.
Dimension (D)
Click one post then another; type the distance inline (accepts math, e.g. 500*2). The first post moves to sit exactly that far from the second. If that would over-constrain the post, a reference dimension is dropped instead (live length in brackets, no border). Double-click any dimension to edit it; drag its line perpendicular and it pops between tidy rows (Shift = free placement).
Set Angle (∠ button / right-click, 2 posts)
Type a bearing inline; the first post takes that bearing from the second (if the first is the more pinned-down of the two, the freer post moves instead — the geometry you asked for is kept). Combine with a Dimension for polar placement, or with an Align to meet a line. Double-click the angle line or its ° label to edit it.
Lock in Place (padlock button / right-click)
Pins a post so nothing can move it — anchor a datum.
Make Collinear (button / right-click, 3 posts)
Puts the first post on the line through the other two. Pair it with a Dimension to set exactly how far along that line it sits.

Span types & components

Panel
Mesh infill in a 25×1.6 SHS frame (2 verticals + 3 horizontals + mesh). Runs over 2400 mm auto-split into sub-panels (intermediate "ghost" posts flagged in the BOM).
Gap
An intentional open span — no panel, just a marked opening.
Hinged Door / Swing Gate
A leaf swinging from a hinge bracket. Configurable hinge position (four bracket slots) and open angle; hinge brackets drawn at the posts.
Sliding Door
A panel that rolls along a 40×80 track (length = 2× opening + 200 mm), shown parked at the far/open end. Options: which post it opens toward, track side, and track / gate-width extensions.
Cantilever Gate
A counterbalanced sliding gate with no ground track. Components: two wheel carriages dimensioned off the rear opening post, an end catcher on the closing post, and an endstop post auto-placed one opening-width back so the gate stays captive when fully open. Options: front-wheel offset, roller spacing, tail overhang, track side, retract side.

Bill of Materials (BOM)

The BOM button opens a live parts summary: posts grouped by material/height/footplate (including the auto-inserted intermediate "ghost" posts on long runs, so the count is order-ready — the number of intermediates is also called out under the cut list), spans grouped by kind/size, a panel cut list (verticals, horizontals, mesh area), and dedicated sections itemising sliding-door track + leaf and cantilever gate beam/wheels/catcher/endstop. Download CSV exports the whole list for spreadsheets. It updates as you edit.

Background floor plan

Import BG loads a floor-plan image behind the drawing. Calibrate: click two points you know the real distance between, enter that distance, and the image is scaled to match — so you can trace at true size. The opacity slider fades it; Remove BG clears it.

Saving & local caching

Your work autosaves to this browser (localStorage) and is restored automatically if you refresh or crash — nothing is uploaded. You'll be warned before leaving with unsaved changes. Save/Load write and read .zonecad.json files, and the Recent menu re-opens recently saved layouts stored locally.

Editing & navigation

  • Undo / Redo — Ctrl+Z / Ctrl+Y.
  • Copy / Paste / Duplicate — Ctrl+C / Ctrl+V / Ctrl+D; Select All — Ctrl+A; Delete — Del.
  • Arrow keys nudge the selection by the snap step (Shift = 1 mm fine).
  • Hold Shift while dragging to lock to one axis; alignment guides + live gap dimensions appear when a post lines up with another.
  • Object snapping: dragging a post or placing a new one snaps to nearby post centres, hinge pins, midpoints and span lines. Polar/angle snap (Settings) snaps drag/placement to an angle increment.
  • Typed move (M): nudge the selection by an exact dx, dy (math-aware). Right-click a selection for Array / Repeat (count, dx, dy).
  • Marquee-drag selects posts, spans, zones and labels together.
  • F frames the selection (or the whole layout); scroll to zoom, middle-drag to pan.

Display & export

  • Units m / mm (Settings); light / dark canvas, layer toggles (zones · background · constraints · mesh), post labels and panel dimensions are one-click toolbar toggles.
  • Dimension all panels (Dim toggle): dimensions every physical panel — each ghost sub-panel of a long run included — showing the true panel width, not the post-to-post centre spacing. Auto-flips to the clearer side, stands down where a real dimension already covers the pair, and carries into the DXF as editable dimensions.
  • Panel bay rules (max panel run, 100 mm size divisor) live under ? → Zone Configuration.
  • Readouts: post count + total run length in the status bar (plus an "unsatisfied constraints" warning when any are broken); zone area shown on hover.
  • Export PNG (image) and DXF (layered CAD: each post / panel / gate / zone is a single grouped object, with optional panel dimensions on a DIMS layer).
